For picking traders to tail, you should not rely on the PolymarketAnalytics-style ROI alone; you want something closer to “return on capital” plus a few other filters.

What to prioritize
For each trader, you ideally want:

Sustained positive PnL

Overall PnL significantly positive over a decent sample of markets, not just one lucky hit.

Edge, not just win rate

High win rate can be meaningless if they’re always laying horrible prices (e.g., buying at 0.80 to win 0.20).
​

Look for traders with good PnL and reasonable-win-rate across many markets; avoid “77% win rate, breakeven PnL” types.

Category consistency

Filter by category (Politics, Sports, Crypto) and look at PnL + win rate per category; tail them only where they’re consistently profitable.

Risk profile / drawdowns

Big PnL with insane swings is harder to follow than slightly lower PnL with smoother equity. Articles on top traders emphasize “long-term positive expectations, low volatility” as desirable.
​

Which ROI to use for tailing
Use both metrics, but for different purposes:

1) PA-style ROI = PnL / (wins + losses)

Use this mainly to replicate PolymarketAnalytics rankings and sanity-check your scraping. It’s a good “PnL efficiency per dollar of realized PnL flow” and matches their leaderboard behavior.
​

It’s useful as a relative sort key among traders on the same platform, but remember it’s not real capital ROI.

2) Capital-style ROI = PnL / total_invested (or equity)

This is the one that matters for “if I copy this person, how fast does my bankroll grow?”.

If you can reconstruct something like average or peak capital at risk, PnL ÷ that capital is the better signal for who’s actually growing money efficiently, like the 2.6% and 11.3% ROI numbers cited for top accounts.
​

In your bot:

Keep your new ROI_pa = PnL / (winsGross + lossesGross) so you match PolymarketAnalytics.

Add a separate ROI_capital = PnL / total_invested_estimate (or PnL / peak_equity if you can approximate).

Rank candidates first by PnL and ROI_capital, then use ROI_pa, win rate, and category PnL as secondary filters.

Practical “tail this trader” checklist
Before auto-following someone:

PnL: strong positive over many resolved markets.

ROI_capital: clearly positive, not just noise.

Category PnL: only tail them in categories where they’re consistently up.

Behavior: no obvious “zombie losses” (huge bags sitting at ~0 forever), which can fake a nice win rate.
